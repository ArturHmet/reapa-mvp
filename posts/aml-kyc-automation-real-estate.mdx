## Why Compliance Is Every Malta Agent's Hidden Time Sink

Ask any experienced Malta real estate agent what takes up most of their administrative time, and AML/KYC compliance will be near the top of the list.

Source of funds declarations. Politically exposed persons checks. Beneficial ownership verification. FIAU record-keeping requirements. It adds up to hours of work per transaction — work that does not directly generate income.

But it is non-negotiable. The FIAU has increased enforcement activity significantly, and fines for non-compliance can reach €1 million or more for serious breaches.

The solution is not to spend more time on compliance. It is to automate the repeatable parts.

## What Malta Law Actually Requires

Under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act and FIAU Implementing Procedures, real estate agents in Malta must:

- **Identify and verify** the identity of buyers and sellers (CDD)
- **Assess risk** and apply enhanced due diligence for higher-risk clients
- **Verify source of funds** for property transactions
- **Screen clients** against PEP and sanctions lists
- **Keep records** for a minimum of five years
- **Report suspicious transactions** to the FIAU

Most of these steps involve gathering documents, running checks, and filing records — all of which can be partially or fully automated.

## The Manual Process vs The Automated Process

### Manual (current state for most agents)

1. Client expresses interest in property
2. Agent emails client requesting ID documents
3. Client sends documents via WhatsApp or email (sometimes weeks later)
4. Agent runs manual PEP check on free online tools
5. Agent requests source of funds letter from client's bank
6. Agent compiles paper file and stores in cabinet
7. Repeat for every transaction

**Time per transaction: 3–6 hours across multiple touchpoints**

### Automated (with the right tools)

1. Client submits ID documents via secure online link (automated request sent immediately after inquiry)
2. System runs PEP and sanctions screening automatically
3. System requests source of funds declaration via automated questionnaire
4. Risk score is calculated and flagged for agent review
5. All documents stored digitally with audit trail
6. Agent reviews completed file in 15 minutes

**Time per transaction: 20–30 minutes of agent attention**

## Key Components of an Automated KYC Stack

### Document Collection

Use a secure link sent via WhatsApp or email that allows clients to upload ID documents from their phone. The system checks document validity and expiry automatically.

### PEP and Sanctions Screening

Connect to a screening API (Dow Jones, ComplyAdvantage, or similar) that checks client names against global PEP and sanctions lists in seconds. Set up automatic re-screening when sanctions lists update.

### Source of Funds Questionnaire

A structured digital questionnaire that guides clients through declaring their source of funds. The system flags inconsistencies and escalates for review.

### Digital Record-Keeping

All documents and screening results stored with timestamps and audit logs. Searchable and accessible during FIAU inspections.

## The ROI of Compliance Automation

Beyond avoiding fines, automating KYC/AML delivers measurable business benefits:

- **Faster transaction completion** — clients complete compliance in days, not weeks
- **Better client experience** — professional digital process instead of chaotic document requests
- **Reduced errors** — systems don't forget to run PEP checks or lose documents
- **Scalability** — handle more transactions without adding administrative staff

For a Malta agent closing 20 transactions per year, even saving 2 hours per transaction represents 40 hours reclaimed annually.

## Staying Current with FIAU Updates

One of the most valuable aspects of automated compliance tools is that they update automatically when regulations change. Rather than relying on agents to read FIAU circulars and update internal procedures, good compliance software handles this in the background.

This matters because FIAU guidance evolves regularly, and staying current is itself a compliance requirement.

## Practical Next Steps

If you want to begin automating your AML/KYC process:

1. **Map your current process** — document every step you take from initial client contact to completed compliance file
2. **Identify the bottlenecks** — where do delays happen? Where do you spend the most time?
3. **Start with document collection** — this is the highest-leverage automation for most agents
4. **Add screening automation** — connect to a PEP/sanctions API
5. **Build your digital file system** — structured storage that satisfies FIAU record-keeping requirements

You do not need to implement everything at once. Even automating document collection alone will meaningfully reduce your compliance burden.
